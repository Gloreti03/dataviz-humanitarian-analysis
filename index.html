Born Displaced.
The Children Left Behind.

Between 2010 and 2022, more than a hundred million people were forced from their homes. This is the story of the children born into that displacement — told through the data that often refuses to tell it.

Forced displacement is rarely an event. It is a slow, generational pressure on the youngest lives — measured not just in border crossings, but in deaths that should not have happened.

This project follows two indicators across a decade and 100+ countries: the number of people displaced inside or beyond their borders, and the mortality rate of children between one and four years old. Where they intersect, a pattern emerges that no single chart can hold.

Scroll through to explore the map, the country-level dashboards, and the deliberate contrast between an honest reading of the data and a manipulative one.

The dashboards

Where the data lives

Three Tableau dashboards built around the same core dataset: spatial distribution, country rankings over time, and the relative weight of each crisis at its peak.

02.1 Tableau · Choropleth

Mapping the decade

Displacement and mortality side by side, country by country, year by year. The choropleth surface is the easiest entry point into a dataset that spans 12 years and 100+ nations.

Who tops the list, year after year

Country rankings shift over the decade. The bump chart shows the order; the dot plot anchors the absolute scale, so visual movement always carries its real magnitude with it.

02.3 Tableau · Treemap

A treemap of the most affected countries, sized by total displaced population in the most recent year. Scale is what gets lost first in headline news — this view restores it.

03
The stories

Two ways to feel the numbers

A pictogram for non-experts: every figure stands for a child. And a radial timeline that lets the decade spiral around each country, so you can see where the crisis tightens and where it loosens.

03.1 Python · Pictogram

Faces, not figures

An explanatory unit chart designed for readers without a statistical background. Each glyph stands for a real magnitude — the goal is to let the eye, not the axis, do the counting.

Built with Python · matplotlib
03.2 Python · Radial timeline

The decade, spiralling

For the top displacement countries, each radial arm is a year between 2010 and 2022. Time becomes geometry, and the rhythm of each crisis becomes legible at a glance.

Built with Python · plotly
04
The ethics

The same data, told twice

The chart on the left is an honest reading: full axis, regional colour, confidence bands, neutral framing. The one on the right uses the same numbers to mislead — truncated axis, cherry-picked years, causal language where only correlation exists.
