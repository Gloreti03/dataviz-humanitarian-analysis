Born Displaced
Survival, Conflict, and the Children Left Behind

Between 2010 and 2022, more than a hundred million people were forced from their homes. This is the story of the children born into that displacement — told through the data that often refuses to tell it.

2010–2022 UNICEF · UNHCR · World Bank 8 visualizations

Forced displacement is rarely an event. It is a slow, generational pressure on the youngest lives — measured not just in border crossings, but in deaths that should not have happened.

This project follows two indicators across a decade and 100+ countries: the number of people displaced inside or beyond their borders, and the mortality rate of children between one and four years old. Where they intersect, a pattern emerges that no single chart can hold.

Scroll through to explore the map, the country-level dashboards, and the deliberate contrast between an honest reading of the data and a manipulative one.

04 — The ethics

The same data, told twice
